Human Ludo

Humanludo

The classic board game Ludo into a real-life adventure with Human Ludo! In this fun-filled, action-packed game, participants become the pawns, moving across a giant board based on dice rolls and challenges. It's not just about luck—you’ll face obstacles, tasks, and brain-teasing mini-games on your way to victory! Gather your friends, form your team, and get ready to roll the dice!

General Rules

  • Eligibility: Open to postgraduate students specializing in computer applications or related fields.
  • TeamComposition: Teams must consist of 2 members (no individual participation).
  • Domains of Innovation:
    1. Defence Tech: Focus on innovations for national security, including surveillance, threat detection, secure communication, and military technology solutions.
    2. Sports and Fitness:
    3. Fitness: Solutions that improve physical and mental health, track progress, and motivate users, possibly leveraging gamification or AI-driven insights.
    4. Sports: Innovations related to athlete performance, coaching, fan engagement, or sports equipment.

Submission Rules

  1. 1. Abstract Submission: A1-pageabstract clearly defining the problem statement, proposed solution, and expected outcomes is mandatory.
  2. 2. Final Report: Adetailed report (up to 10 pages) explaining the methodology, technologies used, implementation details, and feasibility
  3. 3. Prototype Submission: Teamsmust provide a functional prototype during the presentation

Presentation Rules

  1. 1. Time Allocation: Eachteam gets 10 minutes for presentation, followed by 5 minutes for Q&A
  2. 2. Medium: Presentations includes live demonstrations of the software.

Code of Conduct

  1. 1. All participants are expected to maintain professional decorum during the event.
  2. 2. Failure to adhere to submission deadlines will result in disqualification.
